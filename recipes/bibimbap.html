Bibimbap (Korean Rice Bowl)

This Bibimbap recipe makes a popular and delicious Korean meal of warm white rice topped with vegetables, beef, a fried egg, and spicy gochujang chili paste. Traditionally, bibimbap was eaten on the eve of the Lunar New Year, to use up any leftovers before the start of the new year.

Ingredients

  • Cucumbers
  • Vegetables (spinach, carrots)
  • Soy sauce
  • Olive oil
  • Seasonings (garlic, red pepper flakes)
  • Steak
  • Eggs
  • Rice
  • Sesame oil
  • Sesame seeds

Directions

  1. Stir the cucumber pieces and gochujang together in a bowl.
  2. Cook the spinach until bright green and wilted, then drain and squeeze out as much moisture as possible. Set aside.
  3. Cook the carrots until soft, then stir in the cucumber mixture and red pepper flakes.
  4. Brown the beef in a different skillet and set aside.
  5. Fry the eggs until the yolks are runny, but the whites are firm.
  6. Top each serving of rice with the spinach mixture, beef, and the cucumber mixture.
  7. Place an egg atop each serving, then drizzle with sesame oil, sesame seeds, and gochujang (if desired).
